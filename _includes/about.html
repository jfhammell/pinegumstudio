Melissa Hammell is a writer, speaker and community connector with over 20 years of experience working with urban Indigenous communities. Melissa strives to balance her Anishinaabe and European mixed heritage, using her experiences to bring voices from different cultures together in a welcoming and respectful way. She has been involved in the creation of high school enrichment programs, cultural camps, social enterprises and major fundraising events. As a senior consultant with First Peoples Group, Melissa leads projects with a talented circle of Indigenous consultants who specialize in cultural awareness training, relationship building and reconciliation.

Melissa holds a Bachelor of Arts in Speech Communication from the University of Waterloo and is working towards becoming an IAF Certified Professional Facilitator. At the community level, Melissa volunteers for several organizations and is the President of the board of directors at Tewegan Housing for Aboriginal Youth.
