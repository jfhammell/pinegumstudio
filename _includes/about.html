Melissa Hammell holds a Bachelor of Arts in Speech Communications from the University of Waterloo. While at university, Melissa became active in the indigenous community and she continues to work to connect with her Anishabeeg roots from Kitigan Zibi First Nation. Melissa was a member of a native women's drum group, the Good Hearted Singers, and she has performed for the Rt Hon Justin Trudeau and two former Governors General. Melissa is currently the Vice-President of the Board of Directors for the Tewegan Housing for Aboriginal Youth. Prior to starting Pine Gum Studio, Melissa held various positions with the Wabano Centre for Aboriginal Health over a period of eight years, from team lead to director.
