Melissa Hammell is a writer, speaker and community connector with over 15 years of experience working with urban Indigenous communities. She has been involved in the creation of high school enrichment programs, cultural camps, social enterprises and major fundraising events.

Melissa holds a Bachelor of Arts in Speech Communication from the University of Waterloo, specializing in public speaking and group facilitation. She has produced a variety of written material for the not-for-profit sector, from funding proposals and training manuals to speeches and web content. At the community level, Melissa volunteers for several organizations, including the executive of the board of directors at Tewegan Housing for Aboriginal Youth.

Melissa is proud of her Anishinaabe and European mixed heritage. She is a drum carrier, sharing her culture through traditional songs and teachings.
